Pool Rules

1. The Draft

Each pool member drafts between 1 and 7 players from the Masters field. The draft follows a snake format with a randomized order.

Snake Draft Example (4 members, 3 picks each):
Round 1: A → B → C → D
Round 2: D → C → B → A
Round 3: A → B → C → D

2. Scoring

Lowest cumulative score wins. Add up the total strokes for all your drafted players across all four rounds. The member with the lowest combined total wins the pot.

3. Missed Cut (MC) & Withdrawal (WD)

If one of your players misses the cut or withdraws during the tournament, their remaining rounds are scored at the daily scoring average + 3 strokes. An incomplete round is also scored at the daily average + 3.

Example: Rory shoots 74-74 and MCs (+4 total through R2)
Daily average for R3 = 73, R4 = 73
Rory's MC score: R3 = 73+3 = 76, R4 = 73+3 = 76
Total: 74 + 74 + 76 + 76 = 300 (+12)

4. Withdrawal Before Round 1

If a drafted player withdraws before Round 1 begins, the member who drafted that player may select a replacement from the undrafted player pool.

5. Winning

The pool member with the lowest total combined score across all their drafted players wins. In the event of a tie, the pot is split.
